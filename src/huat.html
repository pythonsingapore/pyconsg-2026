8 Reasons To Attend PyCon Singapore 2026

  1. 1 🧠
    Walk out with your own AI "second brain" — and a certificate to prove it. ✨

    This is the big one. Over all three days you'll build a personal learning assistant with Hermes Agent, beginner-friendly and BYOD, and everyone who completes it earns the AI Ready ASEAN digital certificate endorsed by AI Singapore, Google.org and the ASEAN Foundation. You don't just attend PyCon SG 2026 — you leave with something that's genuinely, permanently yours.

  2. 2 🎤
    Four keynotes that hit different. 🔥

    This isn't generic AI hype. Georgi Ker (PSF Vice-Chair) opens with "So Kiasu, Still Kena Replaced by AI?" — part career survival guide, part love letter to anyone who felt the playbook wasn't written for them. Then NUS's Prof Anthony Tung lays out a four-stage model of AI fluency and the difference between using AI and actually thinking with it. Google's Thu Ya Kyaw maps the path beyond "vibe coding" toward real engineering, and Python Asia's Freilla Mae Espinola closes by connecting communities across the region. Worth the ticket on their own.

  3. 3 🧑‍💻
    Learn from engineers at the companies you actually want to work for. 🏢

    The speaker roster reads like a who's-who: Microsoft, Hugging Face, Red Hat, DBS Bank, Grab, MongoDB, ByteDance/TikTok, ClickHouse, Apify, JetBrains and more. Where else can you hear ByteDance's Global Red Team Lead dissect a real 2026 Python supply-chain attack and hand you a layered defence you can deploy that same week? Real systems, real war stories, real takeaways.

  4. 4 🛠️
    Hands-on workshops for every level — from first line of code to GPU kernels. 🚀

    Day 3 is pure building. PyLadies runs Python 101 and 102 for absolute beginners, while the brave can tackle SIMD/GPU/TPU acceleration patterns, LangGraph + MongoDB agent workflows, Google's Agent Development Kit, pytest mastery, and LLM evaluation with Lighteval. Beginner or veteran — there's a seat that fits you exactly.

  5. 5 💸
    Best value in town — community-priced. 🎟️

    PyCon Singapore is a community event, run by the community for the community. Despite Singapore's renowned high cost of living, tickets are only $10 — and they come loaded: an event T-shirt, swag from our sponsors, an easily accessible location right by Paya Lebar MRT, and a weekend-friendly schedule (19–21 June) so you don't have to burn precious leave. All for less than the price of a movie-and-popcorn.

  6. 6 🇸🇬
    Build the future you, the Singapore way — with SkillsFuture in the room. 🧭

    There's a dedicated SkillsFuture Advice workshop run by Lifelong Learning SG instructor to help you map emerging skills to your career and tap SkillsFuture support. Combined with the conference's focus on Singapore's AI-bilingual workforce agenda, you leave with a concrete plan — not just inspiration.

  7. 7 🤖
    Ship something live: hackathon + AI Agents Showcase. 🏆

    This isn't passive sitting-and-listening. Day 1 starts with AI Agent and hackathon announcements, and Day 2 closes with an AI Agents Showcase.

    Your tribe is here — including the women shaping Python's future. 🧑‍🤝‍🧑

There's a PyLadiesSG track, group mentoring carousels, a panel on agentic AI across industries, lightning talks, and networking — plus a bridge to the wider Python Asia community across APAC. The connections you make outlast the line of prompts or code you write.
