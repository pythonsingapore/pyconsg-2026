Opportunity Pitch

High impact pitch for jobs & internships

On 19th and 20th June 2026 (Day 1 and Day 2 of PyCon Singapore 2026), we will run short and voluntary 3-minute pitches during transition interludes across the conference schedule for participants who are looking for a job or internship.

The goal is not to “sound impressive”. The goal is to be credible: share evidence that convinces companies and organisations you are worth hiring, in a world where AI is everywhere.

The Two Questions Pitch Template

What Matters

Answer these two questions

Structure your pitch around the highest-signal differentiators: judgement, responsibility, and real-world impact.

Question 01
What can I do better than AI?
Question 02
What can I do that AI cannot?

How It Works

Short, structured, evidence-based

Pitches happen during transition interludes. Please keep it tight, respectful, and focused on what you can prove.

01
Volunteer for a slot

Slots are limited. Details on how to indicate interest will be shared closer to the event and on-site.

02
Bring evidence

Show proof: measurable outcomes, demos, links, and references. Claims without evidence are hard to trust.

03
End with a clear ask

State what you want (job, internship, domain, location) and how someone can follow up after your pitch.

Good To Know
Respect & community first

This is a community opportunity. Keep pitches welcoming, inclusive, and professional.

Always applies: Code of Conduct

[Optional] Template

A 3-minute pitch you can practise

Use a structure that makes it easy to evaluate you quickly. Keep it concrete, specific, and verifiable.

0:00 – 0:20 | Who you are

Name, role you want, and what you build (1 sentence).

0:20 – 1:10 | What you shipped

A project or experience with context: for whom, what problem, and why it mattered.

1:10 – 2:10 | Evidence
  • Metrics: performance, cost, reliability, adoption, revenue, time saved.
  • Artifacts: repo, demo, slides, write-up, tests, screenshots, dashboards.
  • Credibility: users, maintainers, mentors, references, or endorsements.
2:10 – 2:50 | The two questions
  • Better than AI: judgement, trade-offs, debugging, customer understanding, delivery.
  • AI cannot: trust, ownership, leadership, accountability, real-world execution.
2:50 – 3:00 | The ask

What roles you want + how to contact you after the pitch.

Checklist
  • One role you want (specific).
  • One shipped example (real).
  • One measurable outcome (numbers).
  • One link or artifact (verifiable).
  • One clear follow-up channel.
Evidence Ideas
  • A PR you authored that improved correctness, security, or performance.
  • A production incident you helped resolve and what you changed after.
  • A data story: from messy inputs to a decision that mattered.
  • A teaching or mentoring example with outcomes.
Make It Easy To Hire You

Companies do not hire ambition. They hire proof of competence and trustworthiness. If you can show evidence of good judgement, strong communication, and reliable delivery, you stand out even in an AI-saturated market.
